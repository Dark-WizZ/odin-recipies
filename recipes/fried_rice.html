Fried Rice

Description

Fried rice is a delicious and traditional side dish in Chinese cuisine. If you're used to ordering it at your favorite restaurant, it may be time to give the homemade version a try.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (473 mL) water
  • 1 cup (195 g) medium-grain rice, white or brown
  • Â½ teaspoon (2.84 g) salt
  • 1 tablespoon (approximately 15 mL) neutral oil, such as grapeseed or canola
  • 1 small white onion, chopped
  • 1 cup (150 g) frozen peas and carrots, defrosted
  • 1 teaspoon (5 g) ginger, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon (5 g) garlic, finely chopped
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 2-3 tablespoons (30-44 mL) soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon (15 mL) sesame oil
  • Green onion, chopped (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. Rinse the rice.
  2. Bring the water to a boil.
  3. Add the rice and salt.
  4. Cover the pot and let it cook for at least 18 minutes.
  5. Turn the heat off and let the rice stand.
  6. Spread the rice on a tray and set under a fan for an hour.
  7. Heat a wok on the stove.
  8. Add a neutral to the pan.
  9. Cook the ginger, garlic, onion, peas, and carrots until tender.
  10. Pour the beaten eggs into one half of the pan and scramble them.
  11. Mix in the cooked rice, soy sauce, and sesame oil.
  12. Fry the mixture until heated through.
